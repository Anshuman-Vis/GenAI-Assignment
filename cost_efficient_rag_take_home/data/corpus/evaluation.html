Evaluation principles

Recall at k counts whether a relevant chunk occurs in the first k results. Hit Rate is the fraction of questions with at least one relevant retrieved chunk. Mean reciprocal rank rewards the first relevant result: its score is one divided by rank.

nDCG at k rewards relevant items near the front using discounted cumulative gain and normalizes by ideal ranking. Context precision is relevant retrieved chunks divided by retrieved chunks.

Faithfulness asks whether answer claims are supported by supplied context. Answer relevance asks whether the answer addresses the question or gold answer. These must be measured separately because a fluent answer may be unfaithful and a faithful answer may be incomplete.
